# LOCKED — Brand Identity

## Positioning

LOCKED is a clean, functional gym companion built for everyone from a 15-year-old on their first session to a 55-year-old lifting three times a week. It replaces four apps with one. The brand promise is competence without complexity.

## Core identity

Professional, not gamified. Calm, not loud. Capable, not cluttered. LOCKED behaves like a first-party Apple app for training. A user should put it next to Apple Health or Apple Fitness and feel it belongs there.

The guiding line: complex functionality should feel effortless, and the technology should feel invisible.

## Brand personality

Five words, each with a behavioral meaning:

- **Calm.** The interface never shouts. No urgency tactics, no red badges demanding attention.
- **Intelligent.** The AI works in the background and surfaces insight at the right moment. A silent co-pilot, not a chatbot begging for input.
- **Premium.** Restraint reads as expensive. Space, typography, and precision do the work instead of effects.
- **Organized.** Every screen has one job. Information is structured, never dumped.
- **Accessible.** Large type, generous targets, plain language. Age-agnostic by design.

## Visual language

**Palette.** Dark foundation with warm brown tones and a single orange accent. Orange marks action and progress, nothing else. Warmth separates LOCKED from the cold blue-grey of most training apps. Dark reads as focused, gym-appropriate, easy on the eyes at 6am or 9pm.

**Typography.** System type, large and readable, with a wide range between heading and caption. Type carries the hierarchy so decoration does not have to.

**Shape.** Soft, rounded geometry. Buttons feel like physical objects with subtle depth and gentle shadow. Nothing harsh, nothing hard-edged, no heavy borders.

**Depth.** Glass and translucency act as accent layers, not the foundation. Navigation bars, floating controls, sheets, and menus get the glass treatment. Information cards stay solid and readable. Depth communicates layering, never spectacle.

**Space.** Spacious layouts, comfortable padding, clean card structures. Whitespace is the primary tool for hierarchy.

**Motion.** Spring-based and native. Every animation serves a purpose. Transitions preserve context so the user never loses their place.

## Tone of voice

Direct, encouraging, factual. LOCKED talks like a good coach: it tells you what happened, what to do next, and where you stand. No hype, no exclamation marks, no fake enthusiasm. Numbers over adjectives.

## What LOCKED is not

This list matters as much as the positive one, since it prevents drift:

- Not a dashboard. No wall of metrics competing for attention.
- Not futuristic. No neon, no sci-fi styling, no cyberpunk.
- Not gradient-heavy or glass-heavy. Both are accents.
- Not a bro app. The visual language does not assume a young male user.
- Not decorative. Nothing exists on screen without a function.
- Not manipulative. No streak guilt, no countdown pressure, no dark patterns.

## The gaming layer

XP, levels, muscle ranks, and clans exist, but they sit behind a toggle and stay secondary. They are a mode, not the identity. A user who never turns them on should experience a complete, professional training app. Any surface where gaming elements bleed into the default view breaks the brand.

## The three-question test

Every screen answers these before it ships:

1. What is happening?
2. What should I do next?
3. Am I making progress?

If a screen fails one of the three, it needs work. If it answers all three in under five seconds, it is on brand.

## Long-term direction

Customizable layout. Users rearrange modules like iOS home screen editing, toggle simplified views, and set their own accent styles. The brand accommodates personalization because accessibility across ages demands it. The default stays clean, and the flexibility sits underneath.
